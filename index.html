Dextra

An affordable, wearable hand rehabilitation system for restoring functional independence

Problem Statement

Patients with stroke, spinal cord injuries, or neuromuscular disorders often lose wrist–hand function, severely affecting their ability to perform daily activities. Passive splints fail to provide active movement, while advanced robotic exoskeletons remain bulky, expensive, and inaccessible to most users.

Proposed Solution: Dextra

Dextra is an active, wearable robotic orthosis designed to assist natural hand movements. It enables essential grasping actions—gripping, holding, and controlled release—supporting Activities of Daily Living (ADLs) while remaining lightweight, reliable, and affordable.

USPs of the Product

  • Adjustable, comfortable, and lightweight wearable design
  • Simple, robust, and reliable mechanical construction
  • Multiple control modes: switch, mobile app, and voice control
  • Affordable solution compared to advanced robotic systems
  • Future-scalable architecture using ESP32 microcontroller
  • Compatible with sensors and future EMG-based control

Working Demonstration

Finger Movement

Flexion: Fingers close to enable grasping

Hold: Stable 3-jaw chuck grip

Extension: Fingers open for release

Wrist Articulation

Up: Wrist extension

Stop: Neutral holding posture

Down: Wrist flexion for firm grasp

Control Modes

  • Switch-controlled mode for basic rehabilitation
  • Mobile app control for customizable therapy
  • Voice-controlled operation for hands-free accessibility

Benefits & Impact

  • Restores functional grip for daily activities
  • Promotes independence and reduces caregiver reliance
  • Cost-effective alternative to robotic prosthetics
  • Improves confidence and overall quality of life

Why Dextra Is Different

  • Unlike passive splints, Dextra provides active movement
  • Unlike robotic exoskeletons, it is lightweight and affordable
  • Designed specifically for home-based rehabilitation

Safety & Limitations

  • Motion limits implemented for user safety
  • Supervised use recommended
  • Prototype-stage device, not clinically certified
