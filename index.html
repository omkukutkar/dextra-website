Dextra

A wearable hand rehabilitation system designed to restore functional independence through affordable, assistive technology.

The Actual Problem

Millions of people affected by stroke, spinal cord injuries, and neuromuscular disorders lose wrist–hand function, severely limiting their ability to perform everyday activities.

Existing solutions are either passive (offering no active movement) or prohibitively expensive, bulky, and unsuitable for home-based rehabilitation.

Our Solution: Dextra

Dextra is an active, wearable robotic orthosis that assists natural hand and wrist movements. It enables functional grasping, holding, and controlled release—essential for Activities of Daily Living (ADLs).

User Confidence

Accessible Rehabilitation

What Makes Dextra Different

Unlike passive splints, Dextra provides active assistance. Unlike advanced robotic exoskeletons, it remains lightweight, affordable, and practical for everyday use.

Safety & Limitations

Motion limits are implemented for user safety. Supervised use is recommended. Dextra is currently a research prototype and not a clinically certified medical device.
